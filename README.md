# General Insurance Analysis
Statistical analysis of insurance literacy and purchase behaviour using survey data and hypothesis testing techniques.

## Overview

This project examines insurance literacy and purchase behaviour using primary survey data. Statistical hypothesis testing was used to identify socio-economic factors influencing awareness, adoption, and decision-making related to general insurance.

## Dataset

* Primary survey data
* Sample Size: 331 respondents
* Sampling Method: Four-stage area sampling

## Objectives

* Measure insurance literacy among individuals
* Analyse factors affecting insurance purchase decisions
* Study the relationship between literacy and purchase behaviour
* To identify the factors influencing insurance purchase decisions

## Statistical Techniques Used

* Wilcoxon Rank Sum Test
* Kruskal-Wallis Test
* Chi-Square Test
* Fisher’s Exact Test
* Proportion Test
* Goodman-Kruskal Gamma
* Jonckheere Trend Test

## Key Findings

* Insurance literacy increased with education and income levels
* Income, employment status, and age significantly influenced purchase behaviour
* Higher insurance literacy was associated with higher insurance adoption
* Awareness and affordability emerged as major barriers
